Mathematics Bank

Decoding the Patterns of Reality

Geometry

The Mpigi Town Council has allocated land for the MIST innovation hub. The site is a composite shape: a rectangle (30m by 40m) with a semi-circular garden on one 30m side. The club needs to fence the perimeter with bundles that cover 15m each. Inside the rectangle, they plan to lay 0.5m x 0.5m tiles, but only have a budget for 4,000 tiles.

(a) How many fencing bundles must they buy?
(b) Are 4,000 tiles enough for the rectangular area?
Calculations:
- Perimeter: (40+40+30) + (0.5 * 3.14 * 30) = 110 + 47.1 = 157.1m.
- Bundles: 157.1 / 15 = 10.47 -> 11 bundles.
- Tiles: (30 * 40) / (0.5 * 0.5) = 1,200 / 0.25 = 4,800 tiles.
- Verdict: No, they are 800 tiles short.
Business Math

To fund the club, MIST members produced 50 solar-powered lamps. The cost of materials for each lamp was 15,000 UGX. During a science fair, they sold 40 lamps at a 25% profit. The remaining 10 lamps were slightly damaged and sold at a 10% loss.

(a) Calculate the total amount of money collected from the sales.
(b) Determine the overall percentage profit or loss for the entire venture.
Calculations:
- Total Cost: 50 * 15,000 = 750,000 UGX.
- Good Lamps Sales: 40 * (15,000 * 1.25) = 40 * 18,750 = 750,000 UGX.
- Damaged Lamps Sales: 10 * (15,000 * 0.90) = 10 * 13,500 = 135,000 UGX.
- Total Revenue: 885,000 UGX.
- Overall Profit: ((885,000 - 750,000) / 750,000) * 100 = 18% Profit.
Algebra

A delivery truck carries two types of science kits: Kit X (5kg) and Kit Y (12kg). The truck has a maximum load capacity of 600kg and must carry exactly 80 kits in total to fulfill an order for schools in Butambala.

(a) Formulate two simultaneous equations to represent this situation.
(b) Find the number of each type of kit on the truck.
Calculations:
- Equations:
1) x + y = 80
2) 5x + 12y = 600
- Substitution: x = 80 - y
- 5(80 - y) + 12y = 600 -> 400 - 5y + 12y = 600
- 7y = 200 -> y ≈ 28.5 (In real life, 28 Kits of Y and 52 Kits of X).
