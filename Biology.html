Botany

During a field trip to a farm in Mpigi, MIST members noticed that crops in a waterlogged area were turning yellow and stunted compared to those on well-drained soil. A student suggested the plants were "suffocating."

(a) Explain scientifically why waterlogging leads to yellowing of leaves and poor growth.
(b) Name the process in the roots that is inhibited by the lack of air spaces in the soil.
Solution:
(a) Waterlogging displaces oxygen from soil air spaces. Without oxygen, root cells cannot perform aerobic respiration to produce energy for the active uptake of mineral salts (like nitrates). Lack of nitrates prevents chlorophyll formation, causing yellowing (chlorosis).
(b) Aerobic Respiration and Active Transport.
Genetics

In a village outreach program, a couple is concerned because they are both healthy, yet their first child was born with Sickle Cell Anemia. They want to know the chances of their second child having the same condition.

(a) Using appropriate genetic symbols, show the possible genotypes of their offspring.
(b) What is the probability that the next child will be a carrier of the trait?
Solution:
(a) Parents must be carriers (HbAS).
Cross: AS x AS -> AA (Normal), AS (Carrier), AS (Carrier), SS (Sickle Cell).
(b) The probability of being a carrier (AS) is 50% (2 out of 4).
Physiology

A student athlete at Mpigi High School participated in a 10km marathon on a hot afternoon. By the end of the race, she was sweating profusely and had passed very little, dark yellow urine.

(a) Explain the role of the hypothalamus and the kidney in correcting the athlete's water balance.
(b) Name the hormone involved in this homeostatic process.
Solution:
(a) The hypothalamus detects low water potential in the blood and signals the pituitary gland to release more ADH. This hormone makes the kidney tubules (nephrons) more permeable to water, increasing water reabsorption into the blood.
(b) Antidiuretic Hormone (ADH) or Vasopressin.
