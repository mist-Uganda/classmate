01 Industrial

MIST members are producing liquid soap for the school. They noticed that when they use water from the local borehole, a greyish insoluble precipitate (scum) forms and the soap doesn't lather well. However, when they use rainwater, it lathers perfectly.

(a)Identify the property of the borehole water causing this issue.
(b)Name two ions likely responsible for this property.
(c)Suggest a chemical method to remove this property permanently.
Solution
aThe borehole water exhibits hardness of water.
bCalcium ions (Ca²⁺) and Magnesium ions (Mg²⁺).
cAdd Sodium Carbonate (Washing Soda, Na₂CO₃) to precipitate the ions, or pass water through an Ion-exchange resin.
02 Physical Chemistry

During a lab experiment, two groups react Zinc granules with dilute Hydrochloric acid to produce Hydrogen gas. Group A uses room-temperature acid, while Group B warms their acid to 50 °C. Group B notices their reaction finishes much faster.

(a)Using the collision theory, explain why Group B's reaction was faster.
(b)Write a balanced chemical equation for the reaction, including state symbols.
Solution
a Increasing temperature raises the kinetic energy of particles. They move faster, collide more frequently and with greater energy. A higher proportion of collisions exceed the Activation Energy, so the rate of successful collisions — and hence reaction rate — increases significantly.
b Balanced equation:
Zn(s) + 2HCl(aq) → ZnCl₂(aq) + H₂(g)
03 Analytical

An innovator at MIST found three unlabelled bottles of clear liquids. He was told they are Sodium Hydroxide, Ethanoic Acid, and Sodium Chloride solution. He only has red litmus paper and a piece of Magnesium ribbon.

(a)How can he identify the Ethanoic Acid using the Magnesium ribbon?
(b)What will happen to the red litmus paper when dipped into the Sodium Hydroxide?
Solution
aDrop Magnesium ribbon into each liquid. The one that produces effervescence (hydrogen gas bubbles) is the Ethanoic Acid, as the other two won't react vigorously.
bThe red litmus paper will turn blue, indicating the solution is alkaline (basic).
