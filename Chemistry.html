Industrial

MIST members are producing liquid soap for the school. They noticed that when they use water from the local borehole, a greyish insoluble precipitate (scum) forms, and the soap doesn't lather well. However, when they use rainwater, it lathers perfectly.

(a) Identify the property of the borehole water causing this issue.
(b) Name two ions likely responsible for this property.
(c) Suggest a chemical method to remove this property permanently.
Solution:
(a) Hardness of water.
(b) Calcium ions (Ca²⁺) and Magnesium ions (Mg²⁺).
(c) Addition of Sodium Carbonate (Washing Soda) or using an Ion-exchange resin.
Physical Chemistry

During a lab experiment, two groups are reacting Zinc granules with dilute Hydrochloric acid to produce Hydrogen gas. Group A uses room temperature acid, while Group B warms their acid to 50°C. Group B notices their reaction finishes much faster.

(a) Using the collision theory, explain why Group B's reaction was faster.
(b) Write a balanced chemical equation for the reaction, including state symbols.
Solution:
(a) Increasing temperature increases the kinetic energy of the particles. Particles move faster and collide more frequently and with more energy. Therefore, more collisions have the required Activation Energy, increasing the rate of successful collisions.
(b) Zn(s) + 2HCl(aq) → ZnCl₂(aq) + H₂(g)
Analytical

An innovator at MIST found three unlabelled bottles containing clear liquids. He was told they are Sodium Hydroxide, Ethanoic Acid, and Sodium Chloride solution. He only has red litmus paper and a piece of Magnesium ribbon.

(a) How can he identify the Ethanoic Acid using the Magnesium ribbon?
(b) What will happen to the red litmus paper when dipped into the Sodium Hydroxide?
Solution:
(a) Drop the Magnesium ribbon into each liquid. The one that produces effervescence (bubbles) of Hydrogen gas is the Ethanoic Acid.
(b) The red litmus paper will turn blue (alkaline nature).
